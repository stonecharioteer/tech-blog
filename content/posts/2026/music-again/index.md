I'm listening to music again. I've started self hosting more services on my Proxmox MiniPC, thanks to using `pi` with Codex. It's making my life easier. I have hated Spotify as a service for a while now, I was on vacation in the Maldives and the service broke on me, and I'd trusted it too blindly. 

I've been running Jellyfin for a while now, archiving old cartoons and shows you can't find online, and I thought I should use it for music as well. Setting up the data wasn't too hard. I had the music I wanted, it was my collection from my twenties, and I haven't been listening to music ever since I lost my left ear.

I love the band Rise Against, and I've started listening to their songs for now. I had forgotten so many of these songs, but I can still hum along. These are songs I used to listen to when I used to write (I was a huge SFF nerd growing up).

What is making me listen again? Healing from burnout. I was severely burnt out last year and the 2 years before, and it took a lot to heal. I am finally myself, and Merrilin is a direct product of that.

It is funny. Abinav said _anything but an ereader_. I knew this is what we should build because in my eyes I can _see_ what Merrilin could be. What it _will_ be. My laundry list of things I hate in readers has only been growing and I've been dogfooding Merrilin for over 5 months now.

We even got a Google workspace and emails for ourselves. It feels a whole lot more real, you know? This is how we get feeling back in our fingers. It is not for money, it never was, if you can believe us. Merrilin was just so that we could tell a story. So that I could show you what I see.

And in its own way, it's healed me a hundred fold by making me want to listen to music as I write this, and as I work on fixing the few dozen bugs that are annoying me.

It is about a story, and like great writers tell us to, we will show you, not tell you. This is going to be the best damn reading app you will ever use.
